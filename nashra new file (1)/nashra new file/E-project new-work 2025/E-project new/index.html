About Us

Istanbul Film Festival

This is Turkey’s biggest movie festival held every April. Many Turkish and international films are shown in cinemas. Directors, actors, and fans attend special film events. There are red carpets, interviews, and award shows. It’s a big celebration of world cinema.

Istanbul Music Festival

This festival features classical music, opera, and ballet shows. Famous artists from all over the world perform live. The concerts take place in beautiful halls and venues. The music brings peace, beauty, and culture together. It’s a magical event for music lovers.

Istanbul Shopping Festival

In April, tulips bloom across parks, streets, and gardens. Emirgan Park becomes a sea of colorful flowers. Families take photos and enjoy nature together. The city turns into a flower wonderland. It celebrates the beauty of springtime.

Istanbul Tulip Festival

Stores and malls offer big discounts and special deals. There are fashion shows, concerts, and street activities. Tourists and locals enjoy shopping and fun together. The city buzzes with energy and style. It’s the best time to shop in Istanbul.

Istanbul Biennial (Art Festival)

Turkey's premier contemporary art event, established in 1987 by IKSV. Initially curated by Beral Madra with European focus, it has grown into a global platform highlighting non-Western artists and Turkish talent. Held biennially (odd years) across Istanbul's historic and industrial venues, it's renowned for provocative exhibitions like 2015's censored "Saltwater" edition. Having hosted 1,000+ artists from 80+ countries through 18 editions, it rivals Venice's prestige as a top international biennial. The event transforms the city into a dynamic art hub every two years, with the next edition scheduled for 2025.

Camel Wrestling Festivals

A unique Turkish tradition dating back to Central Asian nomads, camel wrestling festivals are held annually December-March in the Aegean region. These colorful events feature specially bred Tülü camels adorned with decorative gear, engaging in non-violent matches where the goal is to make the opponent sit or flee. The biggest competitions take place in Selçuk, İzmir, drawing thousands of spectators to witness this ancient sport accompanied by folk music, local cuisine, and handicraft markets. While facing modern animal rights debates, the festivals remain protected as intangible cultural heritage, preserving Turkey's nomadic roots. The next major championship will be held in January 2025 at Selçuk near Ephesus.
